What ContractReview is

ContractReview takes a contract PDF or pasted text plus a few inputs (contract type, your role, jurisdiction) and returns a structured summary: clauses that commonly cause problems, things worth negotiating, clauses that look standard, suggested questions to ask, and a rough risk score. The output is delivered as HTML and PDF and emailed to you.

The patterns it flags are common red flags identified across millions of contracts: auto-renewal traps, broad indemnification, unilateral amendment rights, unenforceable non-competes, IP grabs, vague scope. Flagging a clause is not the same as saying it is illegal — and missing a clause is not the same as saying it is safe.

When to consult an attorney instead

The output is best treated as a sanity check, not the final word. You should engage a qualified attorney — not just a $9 review — if any of the following apply:

  • The contract involves real estate purchase, mortgage, or commercial lease over multi-year terms.
  • The contract is an employment offer with equity, RSUs, options, or executive compensation.
  • The contract is a partnership / co-founder / operating agreement for a business.
  • The contract is for an M&A transaction, fundraise, or major investment.
  • The contract involves healthcare, finance, government, or other regulated industries.
  • You are negotiating against a counterparty with significantly more leverage and the dollars at stake exceed your appetite to lose.
  • You suspect the other party has acted in bad faith or you anticipate litigation.
